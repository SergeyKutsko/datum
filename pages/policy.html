Policy

This policy explains basic work principles of 0crat ("Zerocrat"): a project management AI bot developed and hosted by Zerocracy.

We recommend you to read these articles first: Stop Chatting, Start Coding; How XDSD Is Different; Definition of Done; No Obligations; Five Principles of Bug Tracking; Bugs Are Welcome; How to Cut Corners and Stay Cool; It's Not a School!.

Developer

You can talk to @crat in:

  • Slack: start a private conversation with @0crat
  • Telegram: start a chat with @zerocrat_bot
  • GitHub: start your message in any ticket with @0crat

§1. "Election." Zerocrat may assign a job to you according to its own election rules. You will be notified in job's ticket.

§2. "Fixed Budget." Each job has a fixed budget in minutes, which will be multiplied by your hourly rate and paid to you when the job is completed. The actual amount of time you spent on the job doesn't affect the amount of money you receive. You will also get as many positive points as many minutes you are paid for.

§3. "Boost Factor." A job may receive a boost factor, which will increase or decrease its budget. Default boost factor is 2x, which means 30 minutes. Default boost factor for a code review is 1x. You should ask project architect to boost your job.

§4. "Refusal." You may refuse to complete any job by saying refuse. You will get 15 negative points for that.

§5. "Definition of Done." A job is completed when its ticket is closed.

§6. "Ten Days." If you don't complete a job in 10 days Zerocrat may take it away from you. You will get no money and 30 negative points if this happens.

§7. "Impediments." You may declare impediments for a job by saing waiting to Zerocrat. Until the job has impediments "Ten Days" rule is not applicable to it.

§8. "Code Review Bonus." For each code review you get a bonus as big in minutes as to the amount of comments posted to the ticket during the review.
